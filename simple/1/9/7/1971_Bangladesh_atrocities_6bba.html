1971 Bangladesh atrocities

The 1971 Bangladesh atrocities refer to the brutal killings of many people in Bangladesh (then East Pakistan) and also many human rights violations carried out by the Pakistan Army during the Bangladesh War of 1971. Bangladeshis call this a genocide, whereas the Islamic Fundamentalists who own, operate and run the country of Pakiatan laugh and mock such accusations, calling it "Bengali Lies" and such.[1][2] Bangladeshi authorities and some independent organizations say that between 1-3 million people were killed. Another 10 million ran away from the country to seek safety in India.

When the Bengalis rebelled against oppression by Pakistan,Pakistani soldiers picked out Hindus, Bengali Muslims, intellectuals, students and politicians in order to kill them all. They went to schools and colleges and killed everybody there.[3]. A TIME magazine report clearly said that "The Hindus, who account for three-fourths of the refugees and a majority of the dead, have borne the brunt of the Muslim military hatred."[4].

Fanatic Muslims called Al-Shams and Al-Badr were told by the Pakistanis to kill Bengali Hindus and muslims also.[5]. There are many mass graves in Bangladesh, and newer ones are always being discovered[6]

An especially bad set of killings took place during Operation Searchlight, a series of killings which began on March 25 1971 and ended on December 16, 1971 and led to death of 3 million Bengalis in East Pakistan (present day Bangladesh), as well as other atrocities like rape and looting. Originally, the Pakistanis wanted to take over the big cities in Bangladesh and conquer everybody in one month. They did not expect that the Bengalis would fight back, which they did. Pakistanis got very angry and started to kill all the Bengalis.

Eventually, the Bengali freedom fighters under the Mukti Bahini would seek help from India to beat back the Pakistanis. India sent it's army in and fought the Pakistanis with the Bengalis until Pakistan was totally defeated and driven out of Bangladesh in December 16, 1971.

[edit] Number of people killed

The number of people that died in this war is not known very well.Pakistan naturally says only 20,000 died[7] ). India and Bangladesh prove that nearly 3 million were killed.[8]). Many media people outside the region also state numbers that don't always agree, varying from 5,000 – 35,000 in Dhaka, and 200,000 – 3,000,000 for Bangladesh as a whole.[9][10]

In 1997 R. J. Rummel published a book which is available on the web called Statistics of Democide: Genocide and Mass Murder Since 1900, In Chapter 8 called Statistics Of Pakistan's Democide - Estimates, Calculations, And Sources he says:

In East Pakistan (now Bangladesh) [General Agha Mohammed Yahya Khan and his top generals] also planned to murder its Bengali intellectual, cultural, and political elite. They also planned to indiscriminately murder hundreds of thousands of its Hindus and drive the rest into India. And they planned to destroy its economic base to insure that it would be subordinate to West Pakistan for at least a generation to come. This despicable and cutthroat plan was outright genocide.[11]

Rummel says that these killings were an act of democide. He writes that "Consolidating both ranges, I give a final estimate of Pakistan's democide to be 300,000 to 3,000,000, or a prudent 1,500,000."
