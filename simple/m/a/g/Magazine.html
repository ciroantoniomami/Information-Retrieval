Magazine

From Simple English Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

A magazine is a type of book people read. Magazines are not like regular books. This is because a new version of the magazine is printed many times each year. Magazines are a type of periodical. They are called periodicals because new versions of them keep being printed. Magazines are printed on paper. You usually need to subscribe to them. One magazine is Time magazine. There are magazines printed about many things. Magazines are similar to newspapers, but usually new versions take longer, they cost more money, and they are in color on every page.
