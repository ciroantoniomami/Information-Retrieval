Margaret Thatcher (born Margaret Hilda Roberts October 13, 1925) was the first woman Prime Minister of the United Kingdom from 1979 to 1990. Leader of the Conservative Party, she is often remembered by her nickname, "the Iron Lady" and "Mad Maggie". She also directed British troops in 1983 to recapture the Falkland Islands from Argentina, that had captured them for a short time, in the Falkland War. Also, she had the second longest single term in history. She also married Sir Dennis Thatcher.
