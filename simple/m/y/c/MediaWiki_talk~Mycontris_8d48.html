Is this being forced to lowercase in the site skin? Xaosflux 14:01, 6 May 2006 (UTC)

Yes. Archer7 14:20, 6 May 2006 (UTC)

I think this should read "pages I changed" or even "pages I edited", for two reasons: "worked on" is an idiomatic phrasal verb like "get around" and the "edit" tab says "change this page", not "work on this page". --Cromwellt|talk 22:07, 8 May 2006 (UTC)

I think we could do better than that. I've always hated 'pages I worked on'. Archer7 22:10, 8 May 2006 (UTC)
Me too. How about "My changes" (I like that one, seems logical, yet succinct) or "My changes to pages"? --Cromwellt|talk 22:18, 8 May 2006 (UTC)
I just now saw this from link on the Main talk. I agree with Cromwellt, plus "my changes" solves the other problem mentioned about the lowercase i.
I am going to go ahead and adopt Cromwellt's suggestion, if another admin thinks of something better they can change it again, but I think "my changes" (or possibly "my edits", since "new" articles aren't really changes?) is the way to go. --Blockinblox 00:25, 29 May 2006 (UTC)
Thanks for changing this, Blockinblox. I don't have a problem with "my edits" except that "edit" is not in the BE850. But I think that creating a new article is still a change, since it changes Wikipedia, rather than a particular existent article. --Cromwellt|talk|contris 00:59, 29 May 2006 (UTC)
Yes, I thought about it some more too, and feel that "changes" is better for those reasons, and also because it parallels the link on the left, that says "recent changes" (or here, "new changes"), rather than "edits"... Blockinblox 01:51, 29 May 2006 (UTC)
