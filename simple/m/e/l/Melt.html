Melt

From Simple English Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Melt is the opposite of freeze. When something melts, it turns from a solid into a liquid (like ice into liquid water). The temperature at which something begins to melt is called its melting point. Something melts when it is heated and it causes the objects particles to move faster and faster, until the structured particles are freed.
