Miranda (moon)

From Simple English Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Miranda is one of Uranus' moons. It is its smallest, and the one which has the least rotation time. Gerard Kuiper discovered it in 1948 and named it after Prospero's daughter in William Shakespeare's play The Tempest. Sometimes it is also called Uranus V.

The probe Voyager 2 made some photos in 1986. These are the only images that exist, so far. Geologically it is the most active body in the Uranus system.
