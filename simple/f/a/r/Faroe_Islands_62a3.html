Faroe Islands

From Simple English Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Map of the Faroe Islands

The Faroe Islands or Faroes (Faroese: Føroyar, meaning "Sheep Islands", Danish: Færøerne) are a group of islands in the north Atlantic Ocean between Scotland, Norway, and Iceland.

They are a part of Denmark, but have had local (autonomous) rule since 1948.

Wikimedia Commons has media related to:
Faroe Islands

The Faroes have similarities to Iceland, Shetland, the Orkney Islands, the Outer Hebrides and Greenland. The island group, or archipelago, left Norway in 1815. The Faroes have their own representatives in the Nordic Council.
