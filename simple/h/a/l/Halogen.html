Halogen

From Simple English Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The halogens are chemical elements under the second-to-right column in the periodic table. The elements in the group are fluorine, chlorine, bromine, iodine, and astatine. They are very reactive, so they are likely to join with other elements on the left side of the periodic table to form compounds. They are rarely (maybe never) found alone in nature.
