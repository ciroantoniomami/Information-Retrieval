Ontario

From Simple English Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Ontario is a province of Canada. It lies in the Eastern half of Canada, bewteen Manitoba and Quebec. Ontario has the most people of any province, with 11 874 400 in 2001. It has the second largest area for a province with 1 076 395 km². Quebec has a larger area. (Nunavut and Northwest Territories are also larger, but are called territories and not provinces).

The capital of Ontario is Toronto. Ottawa, which is the capital of Canada, is also in Ontario. The government of Ontario sits at Queen's Park in Toronto. The leader of the government is called the Premier. There is also a Lieutenant Governor of Ontario who represents the Queen.

Most of the people in Ontario live in the south, and that is where the big cities are. The cities in the north are smaller. In the far north of Ontario hardly any people live at all, and there are no roads or railways.

[edit] Weather of Ontario

Much of Ontario gets lots of snow in the winter. In the summer, it can get very hot in the bottom parts. In some big cities, there is smog in the summer.

[edit] History of Ontario

Ontario became part of Canada when it was created in 1867. Before 1867, it was known as Canada East, which is a part of the British Empire.
