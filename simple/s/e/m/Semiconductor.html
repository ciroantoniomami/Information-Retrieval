Semiconductor

From Simple English Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

A semiconductor is a solid whose electrical conductivity can be controlled by adding impurities. The electrical conductivity can be changed a great deal with a high level of control. Semiconductor devices are used in computers, mobile phones, digital audio players and many other electronic devices. Silicon is the most important commercial semiconductor, though many others are used.
