Seljuq dynasty

From Simple English Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The Seljuqs (also Seldjuk, Seldjuq, Seljuk, sometimes also Seljuq Turks) were a Muslim dynasty coming from the Oghuz Turkic. They ruled parts of Central Asia and the Middle East from the 11th to 14th centuries. Today, they are remembered as great patrons of Persian culture and for their empire known as Great Seljuk Empire that stretched from Anatolia to Pakistan and was the target of the First Crusade. They are also regarded as the cultural ancestors of the Western Turks, the present-day inhabitants of Azerbaijan, Turkey, and Turkmenistan.

Originally, the House of Seljuq was a branch of the Kinik Oghuz Turks who in the 9th century lived north of the Caspian and Aral Sea in their Yabghu Khaganate of the Oghuz confederacy.
