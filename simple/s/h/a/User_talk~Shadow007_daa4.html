[edit] Governor-General.

As the Governor General has the power of Royal assent over legislation (making laws as well as enforcing them) and is the acting head of state, he does meet the definition of politician.

In a state, a member of the executive branch of government, or the office of 
Head of State, as well as the legislative branch, and regional and local levels 
of government.

By definition, even a child figurehead monarch qualifies as a politician. A royally appointed Head of state is still a head of state and thereby, a politician. Jeffery can not even use the "Is a member of the military executing law" portion of the definitions as Royal assent means technically he is making the act of parliament into a law. And with him there is also the position of governor of West Australia also marking him as a politician. -- Creol(talk) 14:03, 3 January 2007 (UTC)
