St. Paul's Cathedral

From Simple English Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

St Paul's Cathedral from the south

St. Paul's Cathedral is a cathedral in London, England. It was built in 604. The man who get it built was Mellitas Bishop of the East Saxons. In 962 and 1087 was the cathedral burnt down and was built up again. Before this day's cathedral there were four others. But they has burnt down because they were built of wood. This day's cathedral is in stone and it was built in 1666, after the Great Fire Of London.
