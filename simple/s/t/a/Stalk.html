Stalk

From Simple English Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Stalk sometimes means following something to watch it. A person who likes to watch other people is also known as a voyeur. When this is continually done to a person it can have a bad effect.

A stalk can also be the main stem of a plant. Corn grows with stalks, and so do flowers. A good example of this is the story of "Jack and the Beanstalk."

[edit] Criminal Stalking

Stalking in a way that makes someone feel unsafe is a crime in many contries. It is not just one event but an ongoing process that threatens or makes that person feal scared. Someone who does this is called a stalker. The stalker may do this becasue they want to be invovled in that person's life, becasue they want to have power over that person's life, or for other sexual reasions. The stalker can hurt that person's feelings by making them scared. They may feel they do not have the power to stop the stalker. Psychologists say stalking is a way of hurting someone's mind with serious effects. [1] Sometimes a stalker may go on to hurt that person's body by assaulting, raping or murdering that person.

Stalking can sometimes be done on the internet. This is called Cyber stalking.
