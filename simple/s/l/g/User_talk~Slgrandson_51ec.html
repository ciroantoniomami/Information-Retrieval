Hello, Slgrandson, and welcome to the Simple English Wikipedia! I hope you will be happy helping here. You should begin by reading these pages: Wikipedia:Useful, Help:Contents, Wikipedia:Policies and guidelines, and how to write Simple English articles. If you want some ideas of which pages to work on, read Wikipedia:Requested articles or the list of wanted pages.

Even though it is a good idea to research an article (like looking at the discussion page) before making large changes, please be bold and try! Any changes you make that are not perfect can be fixed later. We are also working most on core articles and the most common topics until this Wikipedia grows.

If you want to ask a question or talk with other members, you can visit our version of the "village pump" at Wikipedia:Simple talk. Administrators on Wikipedia can also help you with more difficult problems. You can also ask me for help. The best way to do that is to leave a message on my talk page. You should always sign your messages on Talk pages by typing "~~~~" (four tildes) at the end of your words.

Good luck and happy editing!

If you need any help, send a message to an administrator. They will try to help you with your problems. A list of administrators on this site can be found at Wikipedia:Administrators.

Marknew 17:39, 12 Apr 2005 (UTC)

[edit] "Info req"

Why are talk pages "requesting information" marked for deletion? I thought that was one of the main reasons talk pages existed...

I don't like deleting any talk pages, because they act as a sort of pressure release valve to stem off cruft from the article itself, while its true that its not a sandbox, still comments or questions that are relevant or asking for information IMO don't need to be deleted... Is there really some policy that says questions about a subject can't be asked on a talk page? Blockinblox 18:32, 12 March 2006 (UTC)

I went ahead and removed those "immediate deletion" tags from the talk pages that were just asking questions - however simple - about the article; because 1) I couldn't find any policy covering this, and 2) Looking through the log that shows the last 1000 deletions on the wiki, I didn't see where any talk pages had ever been deleted for that reason; although it's true that a couple of main article pages have been deleted under "info req", because they were merely asking questions. If there is any reason you want a page deleted other than those listed under "immediate", please use the other deletion template, where it goes to discussion first. Thanks, Blockinblox 20:41, 12 March 2006 (UTC)

[edit] RE Ash Ketchum et al

Please don't. Besides being totally counter to Wikipedia:Core articles, it has a very limited audience/importance. Until we have articles on such topics as domesticated animals, fictional characters seem a luxury we can't afford. I purposely kept myself from mentioning your movie articles, as you at least set a notability perspecitve by using the AFI lists, but I have my limits. Regular EN is discounted by teachers as a 'legit' reference source among teachers due to the over abundence of fic artices--I don't want Simple to go that route--PLEASEEEEEEE let's stick with 'real world' articles for now. Compare the detail of en:Brian Griffin (a great read for a Family Guy fan like myself) to the lack of historical detail at en:Igloo. Since 'Wikipedia is not paper', Igloo should be the most informative article on the web--instead it's not much more than a placeholder. Bottom line, if it doesn't matter to a tribesperson in Zimbabwae, we probably shouldn't have it. Freshstart 08:07, 13 March 2006 (UTC)

[edit] RE: A major matter to deal with here...

I have blocked at least one of the usernames, and other admins have blocked others as they've been created. But a regular admin cannot look up the IP addresses that were used to create those user accounts, so only an active bureaucrat, i.e. Netoholic, is technically able to block those IPs. Also, if they are coming from a non-fixed IP (such as all AOL users), it would be against policy (as well as being ineffective) to block them as the blocks would effect non-vandal users. Freshstart 01:52, 31 March 2006 (UTC)
