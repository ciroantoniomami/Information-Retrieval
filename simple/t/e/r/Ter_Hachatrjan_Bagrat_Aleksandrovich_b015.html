Ter Hachatrjan Bagrat Aleksandrovich

From Simple English Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Duke Bagrat Aleksandrovich Ter Hachatrjan (1982, - Kafan, 1981) was Officer of a Russian Imperial Army. He was rewarded with the Military Order of the Saint Grand Martyr and the Triumphant George. One of the highest rewards of Russian Impire.

After the death of his parents, father Aleksandr and mother Sofia, Duke Bagrat Aleksandrovich escaped from Petrograd to far Caucasus. Duke Bagrat Aleksandrovich got married to his first wife Elizaveta Danilovna in 1923. He divorced Elizaveta Danilovna in 1927. From this matrimony he had a daughter and a son:

Duchess Emma Bagratovna (1924 - 2005)

Duke Yuri Bagratovich

In the year 1932, Duke Bagrat Aleksandrovich got married to Petrachuk Olga Alekseevna, Ukrain – Pols panna, daughter of merchant. Daughters from this matrimony:

Duchess Amalia Bagratovna (1933)

Duchess Inessa Bagratovna (1936)

In 1937, family Ter Hachatrjan felt under the repression of the NKVD. Family was arrested and put in jail ‘Bert’ in the city Goris. Duke Bagrat Aleksandrovich was accused in joining the execution of 26 Baku Commissars. As all noble families were Duke Bagrat Aleksandrovich guilty of betrayal and became the enemy of the Soviet State. Under the pressure of local government, Duke Bagrat Aleksandrovich had to take a distance from his hereditary title and all his possessions that were brought from Petrograd. The family Ter Hachtrjan felt under the amnesty.

After that Bagrat Aleksandrovich worked as director and actor of a moving theatre in Amenia.
