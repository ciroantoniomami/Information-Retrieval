Tool (band)

From Simple English Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Tool is an American progressive metal band. It was formed in 1990 in Los Angeles, California. The band took a break after their third album Lateralus because singer Maynard James Keenan was working with his other band, A Perfect Circle. The band never broke up. The band has won Grammy Awards for Best Metal Performance in 1997 for the song Ænema and in 2001 for the song Schism. Tool has of four members in the band: Danny Carey, Justin Chancellor, Adam Jones, and Maynard James Keenan.

Tool has released four full studio albums. Their first album, Opiate,had only 7 songs on it and the album Salival was a recording of a live performance. The band released their fourth album 10,000 Days on May 2, 2006.
