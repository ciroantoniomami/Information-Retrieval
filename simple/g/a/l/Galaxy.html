Galaxy

From Simple English Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

A picture of the Milky Way, our galaxy.

A galaxy is a group of millions of stars, gas, dust, and dark matter. Galaxies are held together by gravity. Everything in a galaxy moves around a center.

Our Galaxy is called the Milky Way.

There are three main types of galaxies: ellipticals, spirals, and irregulars.

[edit] Things bigger than galaxies

Galaxies are grouped together in groups of galaxies.

Groups of galaxies are grouped together in galactic superclusters.

All the galactic superclusters are inside The Universe
