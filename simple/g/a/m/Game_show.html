Game show

From Simple English Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

A game show is a program on television where people or celebrities, sometimes as a team, play a game for points or prizes. Game shows often reward people with money, holidays, or cars. These prizes are often given by the people or group of people that give the game show money to run. The people who give the game show money to run are called sponsors. The first game shows were on radio.
