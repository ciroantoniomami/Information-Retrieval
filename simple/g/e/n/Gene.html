Gene

From Simple English Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

A gene is a hereditary unit consisting of DNA that occupies a spot on a chromosome and decides a characteristic in an organism.

Genes are passed on from parent to child and are believed by many to be an important part of what decides looks and behavior.
