Basketball

From Simple English Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Basketball is a very popular sport played with a round ball that bounces, also called a basketball. It was invented in Springfield, Massachussets by a Canadian professor named Doctor James Naismith. It is played with two teams, with 5 players from each team on the court at one time. The court, where the game is played, is a rectangle shape, and at both endlines there is a goal called a "hoop" in the shape of a basket with the bottom cut out. Players try to score points by "shooting" (throwing) the ball at the hoops at either the end of the court. The players of the opposing team will try to block them from making a goal. Players must "dribble" (bounce) the ball, and not walk with it; they will throw the ball to others on their own team, but will try not to let the opposing team capture the ball.

Basketball is popular in the United States and other countries. It is played in the summer Olympic Games.

Women's Basketball is the same, but uses a ball that is 1 inch smaller in circumference.
