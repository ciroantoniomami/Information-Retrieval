[edit] Redlines

Hello. I have a doubt. There are several redlines requiring to be inked – I mean they require at least a stub to make them glow wiki-blue. Redlines do not look nice. Now, for each such redline, we may not have immediately a full page. A few words may suffice for the time being. However, the content should be comprehensive as far as possible. I am awaiting your suggestion. --Bhadani 16:28, 30 July 2006 (UTC)

Red links are beautiful. Someone who is really interested in a topic will see the red link and hopefully devote some time and contribute a really good start to the page. By "filling in" red links with really short stubs, you fool the reader into thinking there is a full article if they click the link. We should not follow what is done on other Wikipedias. We should make really good core articles. Think of this way... we now have about 10000 articles, but about 5000 of those are stubs. That's not very good. -- Netoholic @ 21:16, 30 July 2006 (UTC)
*Reference: Yes. Netoholic. I request reference to your constructive advice.

I thought over the matter, and found that your advice is really good. I have up-dated five pages:

As and when I find time, I would also try to update other stubs created by me/ other editors. I will touch them only when I am sure to create a a page with reasonable contents. You bought me with your words: "We should not follow what is done on other Wikipedias.
