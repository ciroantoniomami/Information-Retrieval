Bolzano

From Simple English Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Bolzano (German:Bozen; Italian:Bolzano; Ladin:Balsan or Bulsan) is the capital city of Trentino-Alto Adige Sudtirol region in northern Italy. Its population is 98.057 (March 2005) and the area of the municipality is 52.34 sq. km. The region of South Tyrol is mainly German speaking, but in the city there live more Italian speaking (73%). The German speaking population of the city is 26% and the Ladin speaking less than 1%. The foreigner are around 7%. Bolzano is formed by 5 city districts.

Bolzano is an international city with a trilingual English-German-Italian university (Free University of Bolzano), the European Academy (EURAC), a Business Innovation Centre (BIC), an international airport (ABD, daily flights to Rome and Munich), the Fair Area, the Archaeology Museum with the Frozen Fritz (the Iceman called here Ötzi), other important museums, a Tyrolean medieval centre with the typical arcades (Portici-Lauben).

Bolzano is the centre of the "Euregio Tyrol-Alto Adige/Sudtirol-Trentino" and with Innsbruck of the Alps Convention.
