Bill Harry

From Simple English Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Some people do not agree that this article is completely accurate.
They are talking about this on the talk page.


Bill Harry is an English author. He went to Liverpool College of Art with John Lennon and Stuart Sutcliffe. Using the name the Dissenters, they promised to make Liverpool famous with Lennon's music, Sutcliffe's paintings and Harry's writing.

Bill created the phrase "Mersey Beat". He created a newspaper with that name in July 1961 to publicise the Beatles and the Liverpool music scene. He got Brian Epstein to go to the Cavern and see the Beatles for the first time. He also gave Cilla Black her name. He was the first person ever to write about the Beatles.

Over the years, he has been on over 400 television and radio shows in Britain, America, Europe and Asia. Shows he has been on include Good Morning, Good Morning America, BBC News, Sky News, CNN, ITV and many television documentaries and movies about the Beatles. The British Council had him represent them for their Beatles exhibiticons in Hong Kong. Henry has also appeared as a guest at Beatles conventions in Britain, Europe and America.

Henry moved to London in 1966 when he was a music journalist for Weekend Magazine and columnist for the Fleetway magazines Marilyn and Valentine.

He was given an award by the British Academy of Songwriters, Composers and Authors for everything he had done for the music business in his lifetime.
