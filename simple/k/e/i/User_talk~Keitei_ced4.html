From Simple English Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Welcome to Simple! I hope you are happy editing here and being a Wikipedian. Some helpful pages to start you off are Wikipedia:Useful, Help:Contents and Wikipedia:Policy.

If you want to meet and talk with other members, you can visit our version of the "village pump" at Wikipedia:Simple talk. Just remember that you should sign your messages on Talk pages by typing "~~~~" (four tildes) at the end of your words.

We have a special page that describes how to write Simple English articles. If you want some ideas of which pages to work on, read Wikipedia:Requested articles.

Also, I am not an administrator. I am flattered that you thought I was. I am an admin on en.Wikipedia not simple.Wikipedia. If there anything I can help you with on either site, just let me know. -- Psy guy 12:32, 4 April 2006 (UTC)

I thought since I was an admin on EN that I would be able to be one over here fairly easily. I thought Simple was just a "simplified" version of EN, but I have been noticing that it had a different feel and personality. Hello,

you might be disappointed that I opposed your candidacy. However, this leaves you time to find your way around. When writing new articles, I found it useful to look at other wikipedias (like the English one, for example). Take stuff from there, and simplify it.

Also my comment was not against titling, which is a very valid way of splitting up large bodies of text. To see how long (and frustrating) an editing process can be look at Berlin Wall edit history, for example.

[edit] Vandalism

Hello, when you fight vandalism, do not only revert the page. Also put a notice on the users talk page. I put welcome and test templates on the first case, and stopvandal later on. When I put stopvandal, I also usually report them to the admin squad -- Eptalon 10:19, 25 April 2006 (UTC)

IP addresses included? --Keitei 13:00, 25 April 2006 (UTC)
