X-Men

From Simple English Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The fictional characters, the X-Men, are a team of superheroes who were born with special mutant powers, making them different from regular humans. The X-Men characters are owned by Marvel Comics, which publishes comic books featuring them as well as many other superheroes. The comic book was one of the first to combine social issues and commentary into a superhero story, set in a world where people with mutant abilites are hated by those born without them and facing similar oppression to that of African-Americans during the Civil Rights Movement. Unlike some mutants, the X-Men try to be good and show tolerance toward humans and other mutants.

The original X-Men, as created by writer Stan Lee and Jack Kirby, were

Cyclops Scott Summers Male can shoot lasers from eyes

and all first appeared in X-Men #1 (Sept 1963).

The leader and mentor of the X-Men is Professor Charles Xavier, who is more commonly known as "Professor X" and has mental powers. Some other well-known X-Men include

Code Name Real Name Gender Powers
Wolverine Logan Male Has sharp metal claws that come out of his knuckles, super-strengh and invincibility
Nightcrawler Kurt Wagner Male Has blue fur, pointy ears, a tail and can teleport
Storm Ororo Munroe Female Can control the weather, fly
Gambit Remy LeBeau Male Can turn air into poison, hypnosis, also uses weapons like a bo staff and a deck of playing cards
Rogue Anna Marie Female Can absorb powers, memories, and even physical appearances from other people when she touches them.
Colossus Piotr "Peter" Nikolaievitch Rasputin Male Can turn body into steel
Jubilee Jubilation Lee Female Can shoot fireworks from her fingers
White Queen Emma Frost Female Telepath
Psylocke Elizabeth Braddock Female Telepath

Some of the X-Men's enemies include Magneto, Mystique, Sabretooth, Toad, Juggernaut, Apocalypse, and the Blob.


The X-Men characters have since been featured in various comic book series, motion pictures, toys, animated series, and much more.
