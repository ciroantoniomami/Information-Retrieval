Cathedral

From Simple English Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

A cathedral is a large Christian church. It is the seat of a Bishop.

St. Nicolas Cathedral in St.Petersburg, Russia

Coventry Cathedral, UK

Cathedral of Córdoba, Spain

Metropolitan Cathedral in Brasilia,Brasil

St. Alexander Nevski Bulgarian Orthodox Cathedral in Sofia, Bulgaria

The 'Ulmer Münster' (Cathedral of Ulm) is a Lutheran Cathedral. It is the tallest church (at 162m spire) and the biggest protestant church in the world.

Basilica of Our Lady of Peace of Yamoussoukro, also known as Basilique de Notre Dame de la Paix de Yamoussoukro, Yvory coast. It is supposedly the largest church in the world.

Hohe Domkirche St.Peter and Maria, is the Kathedral of Cologne, Germany. It is a UNESCO World Heritage site. It is also one of the most famous churches in Germany.

The dome (cathedral) of Speyer is the biggest romanesque Church in the world that still stands (Since the abbey of Cluny was destroyed)
