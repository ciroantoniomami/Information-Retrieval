[edit] Basic English combined wordlist

You recently deleted absorption due to it being simply a definition article. However, seeing as it's one of the words on the list of 1500 basic/extended wordlist, I would argue that those words should all have articles. I would agree with the stance that, normally, a word that has just a definition should not necessarily have a page. But do you disagree with my stance that all of the 1500 basic words should have a page? Why? I think the burden of proof lies with you, especially in the case of the word absorption which has plenty of potential as an eventual disambiguation page (which is what it is on the standard Wikipedia [1]). And in the meantime, it could certainly be helpful to people who aren't as familiar with English. Please explain to me what your reasoning in deleting this page could have been in light of my argument. Thank you. Tygartl1 21:48, 19 January 2007 (UTC)

Creol, I understand your point, and thank you for explaining it to me, but I now have another question. Isn't the process of of piping all "dictionary definition" words to wiktionary creating more work? Let me explain what I mean. Let's say I didn't know that the standard was to pipe the pages to wiktionary (and I'm guessing most people don't know that since it's not standard practice on English Wikipedia) and I did a normal internal wikilink to a word that only exists on wiktionary (for example, absorption). It's now a dead link. Is there a way to make absorption an automatic redirect page to wiktionary? If there is, I say, let's do it. If not, perhaps we should reconsider putting a Basic English definition on the pages in question and adding the template {{wiktionary}} to them. The point is to find a solution that will easily allow people to internally link to the meaning of the word. I think we need to plan for the future and assume that someone is not going to have the time or energy to go through and pipe every occurrence of a "dictionary definition" word over to the wiktionary. Thoughts? Tygartl1 16:33, 20 January 2007 (UTC)

I'm very new to all this and this is my second day. But the first thing I noticed was the 1500 basic/extended wordlist had words without any entry/article. I then discovered that there is also the wiktionary. And it seems that I have stumbled upon a debate. Being that I'm so new I think its redundant to have two pages for the same word and it would seem the logical step to send or "pipe" the words "dictionary definition" to the wiktionary if possible. But after reading Tygartl1's comment it would be wise in my eyes to have an entry/article for each word and a link to the wiktionary entry corresponding for that word, only because of the "eventual disambiguation." --Stjetters 20:48, 20 January 2007 (UTC)

Also...is this supposed to be so addictive? --Stjetters 20:49, 20 January 2007 (UTC)
