[edit] Bastides again

Hello, that category for the Bastides may not be as simple as it looks. The 'bastide' entry can be in History of France/Cities of France/Bulidings and structures (I created a cat:Bastides, which is exacltly that). The problem lies elsewhere. While most people see Montauban (50km north of Touluose) as a prototype for most bastides, and as a bastide itself, this is less the case for Aigues-Mortes (next to Nîmes), or Libourne (about 50km from Bordeaux). The problem is that those people will be looking for such cities (esp. Aigues-Mortes is rather touristy). They will perhaps look in the Cities in France cat - and not find it there. The other problem we face is that there are 'bastides' outside France, eg. Mortat/Murten in Switzerland, or Andorra-la-Vella in Andorra. For this reason, we should come up with a plan on how to plan those categories... You are the Category guy here, not me. I am of course open to any input. --Eptalon 20:13, 3 April 2007 (UTC)

[edit] Adminship

What happens if an administrator accidentally deletes his or her own user page? Or accidentally block his or her user page? 69.218.230.103 02:46, 4 April 2007 (UTC)

Any page that gets deleted can be undeleted by any admin. Pages can not be blocked, only users can be blocked. If an admin were to accidentally block themself, they would not be able to edit pages until they unblocked themself. If another admin blocked them, they would need to contact that admin about having them unblock them as it may not have been an accident - there could have been a reason for the block. 02:58, 4 April 2007 (UTC)

Note: 'they' is plural. 'admin' is singular, especially 'an admin'. — This unsigned comment was added by 69.218.230.103 (talk • contribs) .

Two notes. 1. sign your posts. 2. They can be both singular and plural dependant on the target, see Singular they -- Creol(talk) 06:51, 4 April 2007 (UTC)
