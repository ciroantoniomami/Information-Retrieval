Country

From Simple English Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The word country has different meanings in English.

1. It can mean nation, a part of the land on Earth that has come together under one government, and that no other government can control. Some countries have mostly one group of people living in them; other countries are made of many different peoples who share one government; still other countries have many peoples who have moved to that country at different times.

Most of the land on Earth is part of one country or another. The United States, China, and India are all countries.

See also: state, nation, list of countries.

2. It can mean a historic region or nation with its own culture and traditions, such as England or Scotland.

3. "The country" can mean places that are far from cities; rural as opposed to urban areas. For example, "I'm going to take a vacation and rent a cabin in the country next month."

4. "Country" (with a capital C) is also short for Country music.
