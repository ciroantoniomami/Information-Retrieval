Darfur conflict

From Simple English Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Villages destroyed in the Darfur region of Sudan (2004)

The Darfur conflict is an ongoing armed conflict in the Darfur region of western Sudan, mainly between the Janjaweed, a militia group recruited from the tribes of the Abbala Rizeigat (Bedouin Arabs), and the non-Baggara people of the region. The Sudanese government denies publicly that it supports the Janjaweed, but it has provided money and assistance and has participated in joint attacks on Fur, Zaghawa, and Massaleit ethnic groups in Darfur with the group.[1]

The conflict began in July 2003. In Darfur most of the residents are Muslim, and the Janjaweed as well.[2] That is an important difference to the Second Sudanese Civil War, which was fought between the primarily Muslim north and Christian and Animist south.

Fighting worsened in July and on August 31, 2006. Therefore the United Nations Security Council approved Resolution 1706 which called for a new 17,300-troop UN peacekeeping force to supplement a poorly funded, ill-equipped 7,000-troop African Union Mission in Sudan peacekeeping force. Sudan strongly objected to the resolution and said that it would see the UN forces in the region as foreign invaders. The next day, the Sudanese military launched a major offensive in the region.

The estimates about number of deaths in the conflict vary widely. According to Sudan's government, 9,000 people have been killed; most NGOs use 200,000 to over 400,000, a figure from the Coalition for International Justice that has since been cited by the United Nations. As many as 2.5 million are thought to have been displaced as of October 2006.[3]
