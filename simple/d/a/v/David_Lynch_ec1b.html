David Lynch

From Simple English Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

David Lynch at the 2001 Cannes Film Festival

David Lynch is a famous American independent filmmaker. His movies include Eraserhead, Blue Velvet, The Elephant Man, Mulholland Drive, and a few others. He was also responsible for a television program called Twin Peaks, his overall most famous work. Eraserhead, his first film, is still his most famous theatrical film. It was recently deemed socially important by the United States Congress. He is a long-time collaborator with Jack Nance, who has appeared in all but two of his movies. He is currently working on a movie, and claims all his films will be done using digital video from now on.

He is also famous for his early short films. The Alphabet and The Grandmother are two of his most famous. The Alphabet is about fear of education. The Grandmother is about a lonely boy who grows a grandmother like a plant from a seed.
