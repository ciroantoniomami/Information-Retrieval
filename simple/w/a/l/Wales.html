Wales

From Simple English Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The flag of Wales.

Wales (Welsh: Cymru) is a country in Europe. It is one of the six Celtic countries and it is part of the United Kingdom. Wales is next to the west side of England. The capital of Wales is Cardiff (in Welsh: Caerdydd). The National Assembly of Wales meets in Cardiff.

The highest mountains in Wales are in Gwynedd, in the north-west, and include Snowdon, which is the highest peak in Wales and England at 1085 m (3,560 ft).

Wales has a population of just under three million people. The majority of the population live in the South and the East of the country. The main languages spoken in Wales are English and Welsh. Welsh is a Celtic language, related to Cornish and Breton. About one out of every five people in Wales speaks it.
