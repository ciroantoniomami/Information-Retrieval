Welcome and anon templates

This is the message which Netoholic (Talk) left for me, and I think that the same applies for your edits:

I think we should go easy with the anon template. Eventually, old IP user talk pages must be deleted so that future users (or those where it's a shared IP) don't get confused. Also, if someone makes only a couple edits, it's fairly likely they will return on a totally different IP and never se the message. Reserve the anon template for users who look like they really want to contribute, say with a minimum of 10 edits. Be careful not to welcome vandals, so check contribs first.

Similarly, I think the Welcome template should only be used when a logged in account actually has made a couple edits. Some people make accounts to have watchlists, or just to check things out. There is a reason why a user talk page is not immediately created by the system.

Thank you. Billz (Talk) 06:26, 18 September 2006 (UTC)

Your signature

Hello, Vector. Please subst your sig by doing {{subst:User:Vector/firma}}. You may also choose to change to a raw signature in your prefrences page. Thank you.-- Tdxiang @ 09:57, 4 October 2006 (UTC)

Seconded. The MediaWiki software normally prevents you from having a non-subst'd template in your signature to keep server load down, to prevent mass-vandalism to all the talk pages you have contributed to, etc. It is not helpful to override this default protection, as the consequences can be quite dangerous to the project. Your current signature can be used with the standard signature feature by entering [[User:Vector|Vector]] <small>([[User talk:Vector|write to me please]])</small> in the signature field, and by checking "Raw signatures".
