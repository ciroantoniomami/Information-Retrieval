Anti-Semitism

From Simple English Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Anti-Semitism is the hating of or prejudice against Jews. One of the most famous acts of anti-Semitism was during World War II. Adolf Hitler ordered his Nazi party to kill the Jews in his rule, in an event known as the Holocaust.
