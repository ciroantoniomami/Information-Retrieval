Arthur C. Clarke

From Simple English Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Sir Arthur Charles Clarke (born December 16, 1917) is a British author and inventor, most famous for his science fiction novel 2001: A Space Odyssey, and for working with director Stanley Kubrick on the film of the same name. Clarke is considered one of the Big Three of science fiction, along with Robert A. Heinlein and Isaac Asimov.
