[edit] Administrator

I'm happy to point out that, consensus being reached on Wikipedia:Administrators, you are now an admin. Make sure to review all the current policies. If you intend to use the "Block user" function, you must keep your email address current and check it often. The big thing to remember is not to use your abilities in any conflicts (editing or otherwise) that you're directly involved. Other than that, I'd suggest starting out slowly, and feel free to contact me or another admin if you have any other questions or just need a second opinion. Try to keep to the core values of Simple:, and remember that we aren't just a copy of en: Wikipedia. -- Netoholic @ 19:54, 30 January 2006 (UTC)

[edit] A couple things

1) I very much support the 'please do not add dictionary entries or years to the "Most wanted" section' preference. 2) I am having trouble figuring out how eagle is 'american POV'--according to the intro at EN, "Eagles are large birds of prey, who inhabit mainly the Old World, with only two species (Bald Eagle and Golden Eagle) in North America, a few in South America and three (White-bellied Sea Eagle, Little Eagle and Wedge-tailed Eagle) in Australia." [emphasis added]. Since the Golden is also found in Europe and Asia, only the Bald Eagle seems to have any particular connection to the US. It seems like eagles are one of the most widely distributed birds (perhaps owls should also be added, or raptors in general), unless you go out to fairly large groupings like Passeriformes (perching birds) or en:Anatidae (ducks, swans, geese); also eagle is on the 'all langs' lists at both EN and Meta. [edit] 'Film' vs 'movie'

Ever since the parent article was moved from [movie] to film I had been thinking of changing all the Cats to match someday. But it didn't seem like a particularly urgent issue since there wasn't much happening in that category. But, now that there is, we should probably decide if we want to change the cats to match the parent, before even more get created. Freshstart 21:48, 22 April 2006 (UTC)

I don't think the inconsistency of having the parent article a different term than it's Cats is desirable. To me it's like having all the Popes in the Cat "Bishops of Rome", all articles on clothing in a Cat named "clothes", the Cat for fiction being "make believe", the Cat for health being "wellness", etc. Freshstart 22:26, 22 April 2006 (UTC)
"Movie" is supposedly US jargon[2] [3], but beyond that I don't really care if it's moved back to movie. Freshstart 23:06, 22 April 2006 (UTC)
