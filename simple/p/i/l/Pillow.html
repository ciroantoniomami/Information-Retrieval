Pillow

From Simple English Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

A pillow is a soft cushion that a person puts under their head when they are sleeping in a bed. A pillow is made from two pieces of cloth that are sewn together and stuffed with some kind of soft material (such as feathers, duck down, or synthetic batting (a soft stuffing). Pillows are covered with a fabric sheet called a pillowcase. The pillowcase protects the pillow from getting dirty.
