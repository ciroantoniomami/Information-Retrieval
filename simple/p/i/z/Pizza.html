Pizza

From Simple English Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Pepperoni pizza

Pizza is a type of food that was created in Italy. It is usually made by putting "toppings" such as:

  • cheese
  • sausage
  • pepperoni
  • olives
  • vegetables, like tomatoes, or onions
  • spices and herbs, like Oregano or Basil

onto a piece of bread covered with sauce; most often tomato, but sometimes butter-based sauces are used. (The piece of bread is usually called a "pizza crust".) Almost any kind of topping can be put onto a pizza. The toppings people use are different in different parts of the world. There are different styles of pizza, including New York, which has a flat pizza crust and Chicago, which has a fat crust.
