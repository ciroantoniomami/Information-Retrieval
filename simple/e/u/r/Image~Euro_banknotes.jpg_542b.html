[edit] Summary

Euro banknotes
These drawings of the banknotes are similar, but not equal to the actual design used by the real Euro banknotes. However they are still considered as "reproductions" under the official applicable ECB act, and so the ECB retains all rights including for the composition itself.
(imported from English Wikipedia article "Euro", then made legal by adding the word "SPECIMEN" over the diagonal of the complete bill, as required by the licencing terms. See below for information)

This is a euro banknote. The European Central Bank holds the copyright on the designs of the euro banknotes. As long as reproductions in advertising or illustrations cannot be mistaken for genuine banknotes they can be used without prior authorisation of the ECB. ECB Reproduction rules.

This template must not be used for euro coins.

Warning: Using these images for fraud or counterfeiting purposes is punishable by applicable laws. This is independent of copyright status.

Note that this creation is perfectly legal as it scrupulously respects all the ECB rules regarding the reproduction of euro banknotes, which require:

  • the attribution to the ECB using the copyright notice provided above; and
  • the image quality must be limited in resolution so that it can't be used to create counterfeighted bills with a resolution of 72dpi at their original sizes; and
  • banknote images must not show more than about a third of the banknote or :
  • for banknotes shown integrally or showing more than 1/3 of their total surface, the word "SPECIMEN" must cover a significant part of the banknote (at least 75% of its width, and 15% of its height), using a non-erasable (not transparent) and very distinctive color and a bold sans-serif font (similar to Arial), so that no banknote image can be used to create counterfeighted banknotes, that could be confused with genuine ones.

This image follows all the guidelines.
